Meals

For Hope

The MOBI is Zimbabwe's very first Mobile soup kitchen,providing nutritious meals and support to women and children in some of our most vulnerable and isolated communities.

The MOBI was launched in October 2021 is already providing its beneficiaries with crucial access to nutrition, support, education,healthcare and long term change.

The MOBI is an initiative that aims to reduce hunger and poverty in women and children, with a focus:

  • Food and nutrition
  • Healthy and psychosocial support
  • Education and literacy
  • Disaster/emergency response and relief
  • Awareness,fundraising and support of homeless artisans

    The MOBI

    The MOBI curently provides in excess of 1200 nutritious meals per month a month for children under 15 living in squatter camps in Hatcliff.We believe that the most effective way of others is to understand their plight and gain their trust before forcing solutions on to them.The best way to archieve this is to spend time within specific communities ,developing a relationship with them whilst assisting where we can. We strongly believe that, through the use of the MOBI,we will be able to develop this relationship and provide longterm assistance to the people that we visit.

    The MOBI is also equipped to react to crises involving at risk communities and provide disaster relief where needed and is a fixture at all Harare's fairs, exhibitions and markets where it is used as a platform to raised awareness,fundraise and sell crafts curios and artwork on behalf of our vulnerable communities.

    AIMS OF THE MOBI

    With the MOBI we are able to reach communities that have no access to alternative assistance through the various soup kitchens, Churches, and charitable organisations operating in and around Harare, with a focus on children under the age of 15.

    The Mobi currently visits two major squatter camps in Hatcliff each week, initially visiting the same communities in order to build up the important relationships required for effecting change.As our team builds up registers for our beneficiaries, we will begin to introduce partners to assist with specific issues within the communities,such as malnutrition,literacy and education, healthcare and general welfare, as well as HIV/AIDS treatment,support and education.

    As the Mobi continues to grow will expand our reach into other communities such as Epworth and Hopely.
